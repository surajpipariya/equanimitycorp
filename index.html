Industries & Synergy

How RDP + MHEC work together to create premium dry mix systems

Tile Adhesives

RDP adds bond strength, MHEC provides workability & extended open time.

Wall Putty & Skim Coat

RDP improves film formation; MHEC ensures smooth application.

Waterproofing Systems

RDP delivers elasticity; MHEC enhances workability on vertical surfaces.

Repair Mortars

RDP increases adhesion to old concrete; MHEC controls water release.

⚙️ RDP + MHEC Working Together

RDP → Adhesion & flexibility – forms polymer film for crack bridging.
MHEC → Water retention & rheology – prolongs working time, reduces sagging.
Ideal ratio in tile adhesive: RDP 1.5–3% + MHEC 0.2–0.5% of dry mix.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is RDP used for?

Improves adhesion, flexibility, cohesion in cement/gypsum dry mixes; essential for tile adhesives, putty, mortars.

Difference between VAE & Acrylic RDP?

VAE cost-effective for interior; Acrylic offers UV & water resistance for exterior systems.

How does MHEC improve water retention?

MHEC hydrates slowly, releases water gradually, ensuring cement hydration and longer open time.

Recommended RDP dosage?

1-4% of dry mix weight, based on required flexibility and adhesion class.

Difference between HPMC & MHEC?

MHEC gives superior water retention and biological stability in cement-rich formulations.
